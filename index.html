MAJOR FESTIVALS
MAHASHIVARATRI
Mahashivaratri is a Hindu festival dedicated to Lord Shiva, celebrated with fasting, night vigils, and prayers. It symbolizes the overcoming of darkness and ignorance. Devotees offer milk, honey, and bel leaves to Shiva, seeking his blessings for spiritual growth, prosperity, and well-being.

HOLI
Holi is a vibrant Hindu festival celebrating the arrival of spring, symbolizing the victory of good over evil. Known as the "Festival of Colors," people throw colored powders, sing, dance, and share sweets. It fosters community bonding, joy, and the spirit of renewal and togetherness.

MAGHI
Maghi, also known as Makar Sankranti, marks the sun's transition into Capricorn, celebrated with feasting and rituals. For the Tharu community, it is a major festival symbolizing the new year, with traditional foods like rice, fish, and pork. Tharu people also perform folk dances, strengthening cultural bonds and heritage.

VIJAYA DASHAMI
Dashain is Nepal's biggest festival, celebrating the victory of good over evil, particularly Goddess Durga's triumph over the demon Mahishasura. Families reunite, receive blessings, and offer animal sacrifices. Elders give tika (rice mixed with yogurt) and jamara (barley shoots) for prosperity. Dashain fosters family bonding and cultural traditions.
